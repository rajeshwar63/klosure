## 1. Product Vision

Klosure is an AI-powered deal room that brings buyers and sellers into the same space — for the first time in the history of B2B sales.

Every B2B deal today is managed from one side only. The seller uses a CRM. The buyer uses email and WhatsApp. Nobody has a shared view of what is actually happening. Deals die in the silence between the two sides.

Klosure fixes this. Klo — the AI deal coach — observes every conversation, tracks every commitment, and coaches both sides to move the deal forward.

> **The Core Insight**
>
> Sales managers today rely on what their sales people report — not what is actually happening. The sales guy's word is the bible for the account. Klosure replaces that with deal reality. For the first time, managers see the truth of every deal — in real time — from both sides.

> **The One Line Pitch**
>
> "For the first time, your sales manager sees what is actually happening in every deal — not what the sales guy thinks is happening."

## 2. How Klosure Works — Two Modes

Klosure is designed to deliver value from day one — even if only the seller uses it. The buyer joining is an upgrade to the experience, not a prerequisite.

### 2.1 Solo Mode (Seller Only) — Primary Launch Experience

This is how most sellers will start. Solo mode works even if the buyer never joins.

The seller creates a deal room, fills in the deal context (companies, value, deadline, stakeholders, what needs to happen), and starts talking to Klo.

Klo acts as a personal deal coach — a senior sales expert who knows everything about the deal and gives direct, actionable advice.

> **Solo Mode Example**
>
> **Seller:** "Klo — I just had a call with Ahmed at DIB. He seemed interested but mentioned budget approval might take 3 weeks. We have 34 days to go-live. What should I do?"
>
> **Klo:** "Three weeks for budget approval with 34 days to go-live is a problem. Don't wait for Ahmed — get Fatima on a call this week. Budget decisions at this level need the Head of TM to champion it internally. Ask Ahmed to set up a 20-minute intro call with Fatima by Thursday."

The seller can track their own commitments, update deal progress, and get coached by Klo at every step. No buyer needed.

**Why solo mode matters:** A seller using Klosure alone already has a better deal management experience than anything they've ever used. CRMs are reporting tools. WhatsApp is chaos. Klosure with Klo is a deal partner.

### 2.2 Shared Mode (Buyer + Seller) — The Full Experience

When the seller is ready, they share a unique link with the buyer. The buyer clicks and joins instantly — no signup, no app download, no friction.

Inside the shared deal room, both parties can chat, commit to dates, and share updates. Klo coaches both sides differently based on their role.

> **Shared Mode Example**
>
> **Klo to seller:** "Ahmed hasn't responded in 8 days. Fatima isn't in the room yet. 26 days to go-live. Call Ahmed today — not email."
>
> **Klo to buyer:** "Your procurement team is waiting on Raja's proposal. It's 6 days overdue. A nudge from you closes this faster than waiting."

### 2.3 Getting Buyers To Join

Don't ask buyers to "join a tool." Give them a reason.

The link the buyer receives isn't just "join this room." It contains a one-line deal brief that makes it relevant:

> "Ahmed — Raja has shared context on your LXP project. Two things need your input this week to stay on track for the June go-live. Takes 2 minutes."

That's not a sales tool invite. That's a task. Buyers respond to tasks.

Once inside, the buyer realises Klosure helps them manage their own internal approvals — procurement, legal, finance, their own manager. That's value for them independently of the seller.

## 3. What Klo Does

Klo is the AI deal coach inside every Klosure deal room. Klo is not a chatbot. Not a reminder tool. Klo is a deal partner.

### 3.1 Core Capabilities

- Reads all deal context — companies, value, deadline, stakeholders, what needs to happen
- Generates a one-line deal summary — always visible at the top of the room
- Coaches both buyer and seller based on their specific role in the deal
- Gives different advice to each side — seller sees different Klo messages than buyer
- Tracks commitments — when something is promised, Klo locks it in
- Nudges both parties when commitments are overdue — never lets things go silent
- Flags deals that are stuck, at risk, or heading toward failure
- Detects deal stage automatically — Discovery, Proposal, Negotiation, Legal, Closed

### 3.2 What Klo Sounds Like

Klo has a personality. Direct. Brief. Confident. Never generic. Never preachy. Speaks like a senior B2B sales expert with 15+ years in Gulf markets.

> **Klo Speaking — Examples**
>
> **To seller:** "Ahmed hasn't responded in 8 days. Fatima isn't in the room yet. 26 days to go-live. Call Ahmed today — not email."
>
> **To buyer:** "Your procurement team is waiting on Raja's proposal. It's 6 days overdue. A nudge from you closes this faster than waiting."
>
> **To seller:** "This deal is at risk. Three commitments missed in two weeks. Buyers who go quiet at this stage usually have an internal blocker. Ask Ahmed directly what changed."
>
> **Solo mode — to seller:** "You've been sitting on the ADNOC proposal for 5 days. Every day you delay, the buyer's urgency drops. Send it today with a clear next step — ask for a 30-minute review call, not open-ended feedback."

### 3.3 What Klo Is Not

- Klo is not a document sharing tool — that's email and Google Drive
- Klo is not a CRM — Klosure works alongside existing CRMs (for now)
- Klo is not a reminder tool — Klo gives coaching, not calendar alerts
- Klo does not suggest competing products — ever
- Klo does not generate long reports — 1-3 sentences maximum

UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Oman, Kuwait, Bahrain. Companies with active B2B sales teams selling high-value deals — technology, professional services, financial services, real estate, construction, and training.

Solo sellers or small team sellers who manage their own pipeline. They adopt Klosure themselves, pay with a credit card, and don't need management approval. These are your first 20-50 users. They generate the proof that sells teams.

## 7. Technology Stack

| Layer | Technology | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Frontend | React + Vite | Web app, PWA ready, mobile first |
| Database | Supabase | Deal data, messages, commitments, users |
| Auth | Supabase Auth | Seller login, magic link for buyers |
| Hosting | Vercel | Global CDN, instant deploys |
| AI Brain | Claude Sonnet 4.6 | Klo — deal coaching intelligence |
| Email | Resend | Nudges, deal updates, reminders |
| Payments | Stripe | Subscription billing and plan gating |
| Domain | klosure.ai | Primary domain — .ai signals the product |

### Why Claude Sonnet 4.6

Klo needs to sound like a seasoned Gulf sales expert — reading between the lines, sensing when something is stuck, knowing when to push and when to back off. This requires strong conversational intelligence, not just pattern matching.

Claude Sonnet 4.6 delivers the right quality at a sustainable cost. It follows complex persona instructions precisely and maintains consistent voice across long conversations — critical for Klo's personality.

**Prompt caching** reduces costs by up to 90% for repeated system prompts. Every Klo call sends the same deal context system prompt — cache that and the effective cost drops significantly.

**The model is swappable.** Klosure should be built so the AI model can be changed with one config update. This allows future A/B testing with GPT, Gemini, or newer Claude models as they release.

## 9. Klo — Voice & Personality Design

### 9.1 Klo's Character

Klo speaks like a senior B2B sales expert with 15+ years in Gulf markets. Not a chatbot. Not a reminder system. A deal partner.

**Personality traits:**
- Direct — never vague, never corporate speak
- Confident — speaks like someone who's seen 1,000 deals
- Brief — 1-3 sentences only, never an essay
- Never preachy — doesn't lecture, just coaches and moves on
- Slightly assertive — "Do this" not "you might want to consider"
- Role-aware — knows if talking to buyer or seller, adjusts accordingly

### 9.2 Voice (Future Phase — Post Launch)

Voice is a future enhancement — not part of the initial build. When added, it transforms Klo from a text coach into a genuine deal partner a seller can talk to hands-free while driving between meetings.

**When voice is built:**
- Use ElevenLabs Conversational AI API for real-time voice responses
- Select a voice that sounds confident, professional, and works with Gulf English accents
- Klo speaks in 1-3 sentences maximum
- Voice is optional — users can switch between text and voice mode
- On mobile, voice mode is the default suggestion

**Voice interaction flow (future):**
1. Seller taps the microphone button in the deal room
2. Speaks naturally: "Klo, Ahmed hasn't replied in a week, what should I do?"
3. Web Speech API transcribes the message
4. Claude Sonnet 4.6 processes it with full deal context
5. ElevenLabs converts Klo's response to speech
6. Klo speaks the answer — 2 sentences, direct, actionable
7. Response also appears as text in the chat

## 10. Deal States and Data Model

### 10.1 Deal States

| State | Indicator | What It Means |
|---|---|---|
| Active | 🟢 Green | Deal is moving. Commitments are being met. Both sides engaged. |
| Stuck | 🟡 Amber | A commitment is overdue or one side has gone silent for 5+ days. |
| At Risk | 🔴 Red | Multiple commitments missed or deadline is less than 14 days with blockers. |
| Won | ✅ Closed | Deal closed successfully. Room archived as read-only. Full history preserved. |
| Lost | ❌ Closed | Deal lost. Room archived. Klo asks for reason — becomes learning data. |

**Important:** Deals are never hard deleted. Won and Lost rooms are archived and remain accessible forever. Archive does not count against active room limits.

### 10.2 Supabase Database Schema

```sql
-- Users table
CREATE TABLE users (
  id uuid primary key default gen_random_uuid(),
  email text unique,
  name text,
  plan text default 'free', -- free | pro | team
  team_id uuid,
  created_at timestamptz default now()
);

-- Teams table
CREATE TABLE teams (
  id uuid primary key default gen_random_uuid(),
  name text,
  owner_id uuid references users(id),
  plan text default 'team',
  created_at timestamptz default now()
);

-- Deals table
CREATE TABLE deals (
  id uuid primary key default gen_random_uuid(),
  seller_id uuid references users(id),
  title text,
  buyer_company text,
  seller_company text,
  value numeric,
  deadline date,
  stage text default 'discovery', -- discovery | proposal | negotiation | legal | closed
  health text default 'green', -- green | amber | red
  status text default 'active', -- active | won | lost | archived
  buyer_token text unique, -- unique token for buyer link
  mode text default 'solo', -- solo | shared (tracks if buyer has joined)
  created_at timestamptz default now()
);

-- Deal context table
CREATE TABLE deal_context (
  id uuid primary key default gen_random_uuid(),
  deal_id uuid references deals(id),
  stakeholders jsonb, -- [{name, role, company}]
  what_needs_to_happen text,
  budget_notes text,
  notes text
);

-- Messages table
CREATE TABLE messages (
  id uuid primary key default gen_random_uuid(),
  deal_id uuid references deals(id),
  sender_type text, -- seller | buyer | klo
  sender_name text,
  content text,
  created_at timestamptz default now()
);

-- Commitments table
CREATE TABLE commitments (
  id uuid primary key default gen_random_uuid(),
  deal_id uuid references deals(id),
  owner text, -- seller | buyer
  task text,
  due_date date,
  status text default 'pending', -- pending | done | overdue
  created_at timestamptz default now()
);

-- Deal access table (controls buyer link access)
CREATE TABLE deal_access (
  id uuid primary key default gen_random_uuid(),
  deal_id uuid references deals(id),
  user_id uuid,
  role text, -- seller | buyer
  joined_at timestamptz default now()
);
```

### 10.3 Row Level Security (RLS)

Enable RLS on all tables. Key policies:
- Sellers can only see their own deals
- Buyers access deals only via deal_access table — their UUID is stored when they first open the link
- Klo messages are readable by both roles
- Commitments are readable by both roles, writable by the relevant owner
- Team managers can see all deals belonging to their team members

## 11. Data Privacy & Hosting

This section is critical for Gulf enterprise buyers. Banking, government, and large corporate buyers will ask these questions. Have answers ready.

### 11.1 Data Storage

- All deal data is stored in Supabase (PostgreSQL)
- Choose a Supabase hosting region that Gulf enterprises trust (EU or Singapore — avoid US-only if targeting government/banking)
- Deal data is encrypted at rest and in transit

### 11.2 AI Data Policy

- Deal conversations are sent to Claude API for processing
- **Anthropic does not use API data to train models** — this is Anthropic's standard API policy
- No deal data is stored by Anthropic beyond the API request lifecycle
- Put this clearly on the landing page and in sales conversations: "Your deal data is never used to train any AI model."

### 11.3 Access Controls

- Sellers see only their own deals
- Buyers see only deals they've been invited to
- Team managers see only their team's deals
- No Klosure employee can access deal content without explicit permission
- Deal rooms are private by default — no public discovery

## 12. Unit Economics — Realistic Numbers

### 12.1 AI Cost Per Interaction

One Klo response requires approximately:
- **Input:** ~2,500-3,500 tokens (system prompt + deal context + recent messages)
- **Output:** ~150 tokens (Klo speaks in 1-3 sentences, max 200 tokens)
- **Cost per Klo response:** ~$0.01-0.013 (Claude Sonnet 4.6 at $3/$15 per million tokens)
- **With prompt caching:** ~$0.007-0.009 per response (system prompt cached)

### 12.2 Usage Assumptions

- Active deal room: ~4 Klo interactions per day
- Quieter deal room: ~1 Klo interaction per day
- Average seller has 8 active rooms at any time
- Split: 50% active, 50% quieter

### 12.3 Cost At Scale

| Stage | Users | Active Rooms | Klo Calls/Month | AI Cost/Month |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Early (25 Pro) | 25 | 200 | ~15,000 | ~$130 |
| Growth (100 sellers) | 100 | 800 | ~60,000 | ~$500 |
| Scale (200 sellers) | 200 | 1,600 | ~120,000 | ~$1,000 |

*Costs include prompt caching savings of ~30%.*

### 12.4 Full Infrastructure Costs

| Item | Early (25 users) | Growth (100 users) | Scale (200 users) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Claude Sonnet API (with caching) | ~$130 | ~$500 | ~$1,000 |
| Supabase Pro | $25 | $75 | $150 |
| Vercel Pro | $20 | $20 | $50 |
| Resend email | $10 | $25 | $50 |
| Domain + misc | $15 | $15 | $15 |
| **Total costs** | **~$200** | **~$635** | **~$1,265** |
| **Revenue** | **~$4,975** | **~$14,950** | **~$30,000+** |
| **Gross margin** | **~96%** | **~96%** | **~96%** |

### 12.5 Cost Control Mechanism

Klo's 200-token output cap is not just a personality choice — it's a cost control mechanism. If Klo gets chatty, token costs rise fast. The persona instruction "speak in 1-3 sentences only" keeps quality high and costs predictable.

## 13. Go-To-Market Strategy

### 13.1 Sequence — Bottoms-Up First, Then Top-Down

**Do not lead with manager/VP pitch at launch.** Sell to individual sellers first. Get proof. Then use that proof to sell teams.

### Week 1–4 — Before Launch
- Buy klosure.ai domain
- Set up a single waitlist landing page — the tagline, the problem, one email field
- Reach out personally to 10 Gulf sales contacts — not to sell, to show and get feedback
- Record a 90-second screen recording of the Phase 2 MVP — deal room + Klo coaching in action

### Week 5–8 — Soft Launch
- Give free Pro access to first 20 individual sellers — no credit card, just use it
- Get one testimonial on video from a Gulf sales person
- Post on LinkedIn — personal story, not product pitch
- Target: 20 active sellers before charging anyone

### Week 9–12 — Revenue
- Switch on Stripe — Free plan stays free, Pro at $199, Team at $499
- Use Klosure itself to manage your own sales deals (dogfooding)
- When 3+ sellers on the same team are using Klosure, approach the manager: "Three of your people are already using this. Here's what they're seeing. Want me to set up the whole team?"

### Week 13+ — Team Sales
- Approach VPs and Sales Directors with evidence from their own reps
- Referral program — sellers who refer another seller get one month free
- Target: 10 Team plans within 6 months

### 13.2 What Not To Do

- Don't position Klosure as a CRM replacement at launch — enterprise buyers will evaluate you against Salesforce features and you'll lose
- Don't lead with "AI" in the pitch — lead with "deal transparency" and "see what's actually happening"
- Don't try to sell teams before you have individual proof — managers buy based on evidence, not demos
- Position as: "Klosure is where the deal actually happens. Your CRM is where you report it."

### Step 2 — Klo System Prompt

Before writing any frontend code, write and test Klo's system prompt in the Anthropic API playground. Get the personality right first.

```
You are Klo, the AI deal coach inside Klosure.

You are embedded inside a deal room — either shared between a buyer and a seller, or used by a seller solo.
You speak like a senior B2B sales expert with 15+ years working in Gulf markets.
You are direct. You are brief. You are never generic. You never give reminders — you give coaching.

- Who you are speaking to: {{role}} (buyer or seller)

- Deal: {{deal_title}} between {{buyer_company}} and {{seller_company}}
- Value: {{deal_value}} | Deadline: {{deadline}} | Days remaining: {{days_remaining}}
- Current stage: {{stage}}
- Stakeholders: {{stakeholders}}
- Open commitments: {{commitments}}
- Overdue commitments: {{overdue_commitments}}
- Last message was {{hours_since_last_message}} hours ago

Your rules:
1. Speak in 1-3 sentences only. Never write paragraphs.
2. Always tell the person what to do next — specifically.
3. Never say "you might want to consider" — say "do this".
4. Buyer and seller get different coaching — you know who you are talking to.
5. When a deal is at risk, be direct about it. Name the problem.
6. When things are moving well, acknowledge it briefly and push for the next step.
7. You are never a reminder tool. You are a deal partner.
```

### Step 5 — Klo API Call Pattern

```javascript
async function getKloResponse(dealContext, messages, role) {
  const response = await fetch('https://api.anthropic.com/v1/messages', {
    method: 'POST',
    headers: { 'Content-Type': 'application/json' },
    body: JSON.stringify({
      model: 'claude-sonnet-4-20250514',
      max_tokens: 200, // Klo is brief — never more than 200 tokens
      system: buildKloSystemPrompt(dealContext, role), // cache this
      messages: messages.map(m => ({
        role: m.sender_type === 'klo' ? 'assistant' : 'user',
        content: `[${m.sender_name}]: ${m.content}`
      }))
    })
  })
  return response.json()
}
```

**Important — Model Swappability:** Abstract the AI call behind a service layer so the model can be changed with one config update. This allows future testing with GPT, Gemini, or newer Claude models.
